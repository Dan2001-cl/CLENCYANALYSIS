About Me

I’m Daniel Clancy Faust Nzenguet-A-Nzenguet, a passionate Junior Data Analyst and aspiring Development Economist, driven by a deep commitment to economic empowerment through job creation. With a background in Financial Economics and practical experience at the Rwanda Revenue Authority, I’ve developed strong analytical skills in financial reporting, tax compliance, and econometric modeling. What makes me unique is my ability to merge rigorous data analysis with a human-centered mission. I specialize in tools like Stata, SPSS, Excel, Python, Tableau, and Power BI, which I use to extract meaningful insights from economic and financial data. My academic research explored the impact of inflation on stock price volatility and exchange rate dynamics in Rwanda—grounding me in real-world economic challenges faced by developing markets. I’m most passionate about using data-driven insights to influence policies that drive sustainable development. That’s why I’ve chosen to specialize in Development Economics, a field that empowers me to craft evidence-based solutions for unemployment and inequality. My goal is to work as a Development Economist or Policy Analyst within a national or international development agency, helping design strategies that fuel entrepreneurship, support SMEs, and create meaningful job opportunities,especially for youth and marginalized communities. Ultimately, I believe that data is not just numbers, it’s a powerful tool for social change. And through my career, I aim to bridge the gap between analysis and action to build inclusive, resilient economies from the ground up.
